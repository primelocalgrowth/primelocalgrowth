## 2. Core Systems

### The Visibility Systems Framework™

Our proprietary framework consists of four integrated pillars:

**Discovery & Analysis** - We audit the complete visibility landscape and quantify the cost of invisibility. This includes market position analysis, competitive intelligence, revenue impact assessment, and opportunity mapping.

**Boutique Service:** We limit to 1-2 clients per month to ensure quality and customization.

## 3. Client Onboarding Process

### Phase 1: Initial Contact (Day 1)

When a prospect contacts Prime Local Growth through the website, email, or referral, the initial response should be personalized and strategic.

**Your Response:**
- Thank them for reaching out
- Acknowledge their specific challenge (reference their message)
- Ask qualifying questions about their business
- Suggest a brief discovery call
- Include link to calendar for scheduling

**Qualifying Questions:**
- What's your biggest visibility challenge right now?
- How many customers are you currently acquiring per month?
- What's your revenue goal for the next 12 months?
- Have you worked with other agencies before?
- What's your timeline for seeing results?

### Phase 2: Discovery Call (Day 3-5)

Schedule a 30-minute discovery call to understand the prospect's situation and determine fit.

**Call Structure:**
- Introduction and rapport building (5 min)
- Business background and goals (10 min)
- Current visibility challenges (10 min)
- Next steps and expectations (5 min)

**During the Call:**
- Listen more than you talk
- Take detailed notes
- Ask follow-up questions
- Understand their real pain points
- Identify revenue opportunities
- Determine if there's a good fit

**After the Call:**
- Send thank you email
- Include brief summary of discussion
- Outline next steps
- Provide timeline for proposal

### Phase 3: Market Research (Day 6-7)

Before creating a proposal, conduct thorough market research on the prospect's industry and market.

**Research Tasks:**
- Analyze their industry and market trends
- Research their top 3 competitors
- Analyze their current Google Business Profile
- Review their social media presence
- Identify visibility gaps and opportunities
- Estimate revenue potential

**Deliverable:** Market research summary to inform proposal

### Phase 4: Proposal Creation (Day 8-10)

Create a custom proposal based on research and discovery call insights.

**Proposal Contents:**
- Executive summary
- Current situation analysis
- Recommended visibility system
- Implementation timeline
- Expected results and ROI
- Investment and pricing
- Next steps

**Proposal Format:**
- Professional Google Docs or PDF
- Personalized with client name and business details
- Specific recommendations (not generic)
- Clear ROI projections
- Easy to understand and compelling

### Phase 5: Proposal Presentation (Day 11-14)

Present the proposal to the prospect and address any questions.

**Presentation Approach:**
- Schedule 30-minute call
- Walk through proposal together
- Explain the framework and strategy
- Address questions and concerns
- Discuss timeline and expectations
- Ask for decision timeline

**Closing Technique:**
- Emphasize the revenue opportunity
- Highlight the custom nature of the approach
- Reference case studies with similar businesses
- Offer to start immediately if approved
- Ask for decision within 1 week

### Phase 6: Onboarding (Upon Approval)

Once the client approves, begin the formal onboarding process.

**Onboarding Steps:**
1. Send welcome email with project timeline
2. Create client folder in Google Drive
3. Send Google Onboarding Form link
4. Schedule kickoff meeting
5. Gather all necessary information
6. Set up communication cadence
7. Begin market research and analysis

**Onboarding Meeting:**
- Confirm project scope and timeline
- Establish communication preferences
- Set success metrics and goals
- Discuss expectations and deliverables
- Answer any remaining questions
- Outline first 30 days

## 5. Sales & Business Development

### Lead Generation Sources

**Referrals:** Ask satisfied clients for referrals. Offer incentive if appropriate.

**LinkedIn:** Build authority through thought leadership content and strategic outreach.

**Website:** Optimize website for local search and conversion.

**Networking:** Attend local business events and build relationships.

**Partnerships:** Partner with complementary service providers (accountants, web designers, etc.).

**Content Marketing:** Create valuable content that attracts ideal clients.

### Sales Process

**Qualification:** Determine if prospect is a good fit based on budget, timeline, and goals.

**Discovery:** Understand their situation, challenges, and goals through conversation.

**Research:** Conduct market research to inform proposal.

**Proposal:** Create custom proposal with specific recommendations and ROI projections.

**Presentation:** Present proposal and address questions.

**Closing:** Ask for decision and move to onboarding.

### Handling Objections

**"We're already working with another agency"**
- Offer to audit their current efforts
- Identify gaps and opportunities
- Propose supplementing or replacing current approach
- Reference case studies of successful transitions

**"We need to think about it"**
- Respect their timeline
- Offer to answer any questions
- Suggest scheduling follow-up call
- Send additional resources or case studies

**"We don't have budget right now"**
- Discuss flexible payment options
- Propose phased approach
- Suggest starting with one channel
- Offer to revisit in future

**"We're not sure about ROI"**
- Share case studies with similar businesses
- Show ROI calculations
- Offer performance guarantee if appropriate
- Discuss risk mitigation

## 6. Financial Management

### Pricing Strategy

**Project-Based Pricing:** Price based on scope, complexity, and expected revenue impact.

**Typical Project Range:** $3,000-$15,000 depending on scope.

**Payment Terms:** 50% upfront, 50% upon completion (or monthly installments).

**Pricing Factors:**
- Current visibility level
- Market competition
- Revenue opportunity
- Project scope
- Timeline
- Client budget

### Invoicing

**Invoice Timing:** Send invoice immediately after project completion or at agreed milestones.

**Invoice Format:** Use Wave invoicing system with professional templates.

**Payment Methods:** Bank transfer, credit card, check.

**Payment Terms:** Net 30 (due within 30 days of invoice date).

**Follow-Up:** Send payment reminders 3 days before due date and 7 days after if unpaid.

### Financial Tracking

**Monthly Financial Report:** Track invoices, payments, revenue, and expenses.

**Quarterly Review:** Analyze profitability, cash flow, and business health.

**Annual Planning:** Project revenue, plan for growth, identify opportunities.

## 10. Troubleshooting Guide

### Client Communication Issues

**Problem:** Client not responding to emails
**Solution:** Try calling, send follow-up email, schedule meeting

**Problem:** Client unhappy with results
**Solution:** Review metrics, adjust strategy, increase effort, schedule call to discuss

**Problem:** Scope creep
**Solution:** Document original scope, discuss additional work, adjust pricing if needed

### Project Delivery Issues

**Problem:** Behind schedule
**Solution:** Assess blockers, adjust timeline, increase resources, communicate with client

**Problem:** Not seeing expected results
**Solution:** Review strategy, adjust tactics, increase effort, analyze competition

**Problem:** Technical issues
**Solution:** Troubleshoot systematically, document issue, escalate if needed

### Financial Issues

**Problem:** Invoice not paid
**Solution:** Send reminder, follow up with call, discuss payment options

**Problem:** Underestimated project scope
**Solution:** Document actual work, discuss with client, adjust pricing if needed

**Problem:** Cash flow issues
**Solution:** Accelerate invoicing, negotiate payment terms, plan ahead
