The 5 Steps

Step 1: Go to Your Business Profile

This takes about 60 seconds.

1
Visit Google Business
Go to: google.com/business
(Make sure you're signed in with your business Google account)
  • Open this link in your web browser
  • Sign in with the email that owns your business
  • You'll see your profile dashboard
Pro Tip: Use the same Google account you've always used for your business. Don't create a new one.
Don't see your profile? It might not be created yet, or you might be signed into the wrong Google account. Check the email address in the top right corner.
✓
You're ready for Step 2.
The 5 Steps

Step 2: Find "Users and Permissions"

This is where you control access.

2
Locate Access Settings
In your profile dashboard:
  • Look at the left menu (the sidebar)
  • Scroll down until you see "Users and permissions"
  • Click it
  • You're now in the access management area
What you'll see: A list of anyone currently with access to your profile. Right now, it's probably just you.
Can't find it? Google occasionally moves menu items. Try using the search function in the menu (usually top left) and search for "permissions."
✓
Ready for Step 3.

3

Step 4: Select "Manager" Permission

This is the critical step. Choose correctly.

4
Grant Manager Access
In the permissions dropdown:
  • Click the dropdown menu
  • Select "Manager" (NOT "Owner")
  • Review the email one more time
  • Click "Invite" or "Send"
This is critical: Choose "Manager" from the dropdown. "Owner" gives too much control. "Manager" is exactly what we need.
