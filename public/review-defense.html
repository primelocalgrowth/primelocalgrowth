Questions

Straight answers

Can fake or malicious Google reviews actually be removed?

Yes — but it requires knowing exactly what policy was violated and how to document it properly. Google removes reviews that violate their policies: spam, fake content, conflict of interest, off-topic content, and others. We identify the violation, submit the flag with context, and follow up. Removal isn't guaranteed, but correctly flagged policy violations are removed far more often than most business owners realize.

Does responding to reviews really help my Google ranking?

Yes. Google's own documentation cites review responses as a ranking signal for local search. Beyond ranking, the response is visible to every future customer who reads that review — it's arguably the most public thing you can do. Businesses with high response rates consistently outrank those that ignore reviews, even with similar overall ratings.

How do you write responses without sounding like it's not me?

Onboarding includes a short voice brief — a few questions about how your business communicates, words you'd use, and how you typically handle complaints. Every response is written to sound like you on a good day, not a generic business. Most clients can't tell the difference between their own responses and ours.
